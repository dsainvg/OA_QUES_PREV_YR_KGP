### Q1. Dealing with Unrealistic Customer Expectations
**Topic:** `client-management`, `expectation-setting`  

Your team realized that unrealistic customer expectations were being set in the sales process, leading to over-servicing. Over-servicing is affecting your organization's growth due to time spent doing unpaid work. Which of the following would you choose in this situation?

**Options:**
- A) Roll out an incentive program for the team to stop overservicing.
- B) Start charging all existing customers for all the over-service, thereby increasing revenues.
- C) Implement an overage policy to set service limits and provide a framework for exception cases.
- D) Deny overservicing to any customer going forward with immediate effect.

**Correct Answer:** C) Implement an overage policy to set service limits and provide a framework for exception cases.
*Explanation:* Implementing a structured overage policy sets professional boundaries, defines service limits clearly, and provides a systematic way to handle exceptions. This protects the company from unpaid work while maintaining customer trust, rather than abruptly denying service or penalizing the team.

#### Q21. Angle of Elevation & Speed Ratio
**Topic:** `trigonometry`, `relative-speed`  

There are two towers AB and CD (A and C being their tops and B and D being their feet). The angle of elevation of C from B is 30° and the angle of elevation of A from D is 60°. An eagle and a kite start flying at the same moment from the tops of towers AB and CD respectively towards the foot of the other tower along a straight line and at uniform speeds. If they meet at one point along their flight, find the ratio of their speeds.

**Options:**
- A) $\sqrt{3} : 1$
- B) $3\sqrt{3} : 1$
- C) $3\sqrt{3} : 2$
- D) $3 : 1$

**Correct Answer:** B) $3\sqrt{3} : 1$

**Solution:**
Let the distance between the feet of the towers, $BD = x$.
1. **Height of Tower CD:**
   $$\tan 30^\circ = \frac{CD}{x} \implies CD = \frac{x}{\sqrt{3}}$$
2. **Height of Tower AB:**
   $$\tan 60^\circ = \frac{AB}{x} \implies AB = x\sqrt{3}$$

Let B be at origin $(0, 0)$ and D be at $(x, 0)$ in Cartesian coordinates.
* Top of tower AB: $A(0, x\sqrt{3})$
* Top of tower CD: $C(x, \frac{x}{\sqrt{3}})$
* Path of the eagle: Line segment AD connecting $A(0, x\sqrt{3})$ and $D(x, 0)$.
  $$\text{Equation of AD: } y = -\sqrt{3}x_{coord} + x\sqrt{3}$$
* Path of the kite: Line segment CB connecting $C(x, \frac{x}{\sqrt{3}})$ and $B(0, 0)$.
  $$\text{Equation of CB: } y = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}x_{coord}$$

They meet at the intersection point $P(x_p, y_p)$:
$$\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} x_p = -\sqrt{3}x_p + x\sqrt{3} \implies x_p = -3x_p + 3x \implies 4x_p = 3x \implies x_p = \frac{3}{4}x$$
$$y_p = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}x$$

3. **Distances Flown:**
   * Eagle distance ($d_1$ from $A$ to $P$):
     $$d_1 = \sqrt{\left(\frac{3}{4}x\right)^2 + \left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}x - x\sqrt{3}\right)^2} = \sqrt{\frac{9}{16}x^2 + \frac{27}{16}x^2} = \sqrt{\frac{36}{16}x^2} = \frac{3}{2}x$$
   * Kite distance ($d_2$ from $C$ to $P$):
     $$d_2 = \sqrt{\left(\frac{3}{4}x - x\right)^2 + \left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}x - \frac{x}{\sqrt{3}}\right)^2} = \sqrt{\frac{1}{16}x^2 + \frac{3}{144}x^2} = \sqrt{\frac{1}{12}x^2} = \frac{x}{2\sqrt{3}}$$

Since they started at the same time and met, their speeds $v_1$ and $v_2$ are proportional to the distances:
$$\frac{v_1}{v_2} = \frac{d_1}{d_2} = \frac{\frac{3}{2}x}{\frac{x}{2\sqrt{3}}} = \frac{3}{2} \times 2\sqrt{3} = 3\sqrt{3} : 1$$

#### Q22. Average Weight Replacement
**Topic:** `averages`  

The average weight of 10 students is increased by half a kg when one of the students weighing 50 kg is replaced by a new student. Find the weight of the new student.

**Options:**
- A) 55 kg
- B) 45 kg
- C) 60 kg
- D) 40 kg

**Correct Answer:** A) 55 kg

**Solution:**
Let the original sum of weights of 10 students be $S$.
$$\text{New Sum} = S - 50 + W_{new}$$
Since average increases by $0.5$ kg:
$$\frac{S - 50 + W_{new}}{10} = \frac{S}{10} + 0.5 \implies S - 50 + W_{new} = S + 5 \implies W_{new} = 55 \text{ kg}$$

#### Q23. Compounded Installments
**Topic:** `compound-interest`, `installments`  

An LCD is available for Rs 25000 cash payment or for Rs 3500 cash down payment and three equal annual installments. If the shopkeeper charges interest at the rate of 12% per annum compounded annually, calculate the approximate amount of each installment.

**Options:**
- A) Rs 8895
- B) Rs 8952
- C) Rs 8859
- D) Rs 8592

**Correct Answer:** B) Rs 8952

**Solution:**
* Principal outstanding $P = 25000 - 3500 = 21500$.
* Rate of interest $r = 12\% = 0.12$.
Let the annual installment be $x$.
$$P = \frac{x}{1+r} + \frac{x}{(1+r)^2} + \frac{x}{(1+r)^3}$$
$$21500 = x \left[ \frac{1}{1.12} + \frac{1}{1.12^2} + \frac{1}{1.12^3} \right]$$
$$21500 = x [0.89286 + 0.79719 + 0.71178] = 2.40183 x$$
$$x = \frac{21500}{2.40183} \approx 8951.5 \approx \text{Rs } 8952$$

#### Q24. Profit and Cost under Seasonal Sale
**Topic:** `profit-and-loss`  

A watch dealer incurs an expense of Rs. 150 for producing every watch. He also incurs an additional expenditure of Rs. 30,000, which is independent of the number of watches produced. If he is able to sell a watch during the season, he sells it for Rs. 250. If he fails to do so, he has to sell each watch for Rs. 100.
If he is able to sell only 1200 out of the 1500 watches he has made in the season, then in the season he has made a profit of:

**Options:**
- A) Rs. 75,000
- B) Rs. 45,000
- C) Rs. 90,000
- D) Rs. 60,000

**Correct Answer:** A) Rs. 75,000

**Solution:**
1. **Total Cost (TC):**
   $$TC = 1500 \times 150 + 30000 = 225000 + 30000 = \text{Rs. } 255,000$$
2. **Total Revenue (TR):**
   * 1200 watches sold during the season at Rs. 250 each.
   * $1500 - 1200 = 300$ watches sold out of season at Rs. 100 each.
   $$TR = 1200 \times 250 + 300 \times 100 = 300000 + 30000 = \text{Rs. } 330,000$$
3. **Profit:**
   $$\text{Profit} = TR - TC = 330000 - 255000 = \text{Rs. } 75,000$$

- A) 16
- B) 18
- C) 32
- D) 20

**Correct Answer:** C) 32

**Solution:**

$$D = k\sqrt{R} \text{ (where } k \text{ is constant)}$$

$$D = 42 - 32 = 10^\circ\text{C}, \quad R = 25 \text{ mm}$$
$$10 = k\sqrt{25} \implies 5k = 10 \implies k = 2$$
On May 16th:
$$R = 36 \text{ mm}, \quad \text{Previous temp } (T_{15}) = 44^\circ\text{C}$$
$$D = 2\sqrt{36} = 2 \times 6 = 12^\circ\text{C}$$
$$\text{Temperature on May 16th} = 44 - 12 = 32^\circ\text{C}$$

#### Q28. Detour & Speed Ratios

Raj's home is 10 km from his office. He started walking at a uniform speed to reach his office exactly on time, planning to walk for 2 hours. **Options:**
- A) 20%
- B) 32%
- C) 36%
- D) 25%

**Correct Answer:** D) 25%

**Solution:**

### Q64. Inside-Outside Circle Puzzle
**Topic:** `number-puzzle`  

*Refer to the double-circle node puzzle.*
* Top Node: (outside=5, inside=2)
* Right Node: (outside=26, inside=5)
* Bottom Node: (outside=50, inside=7)
* Left Node: (outside=37, inside=?)

**Options:**
- A) 7
- B) 4
- C) 8
- D) 6

**Correct Answer:** D) 6

**Solution:**
The relation is: $\text{Outside} = \text{Inside}^2 + 1$.
* Top: $2^2 + 1 = 5$
* Right: $5^2 + 1 = 26$
* Bottom: $7^2 + 1 = 50$
* Left: $?^2 + 1 = 37 \implies ?^2 = 36 \implies ? = 6$.

### Q65. Critical Reasoning: Milk Route

Ram has a milk route. Each morning, he delivers milk to 20 customers in his neighborhood. It takes Ram 50 minutes to deliver all the milk.

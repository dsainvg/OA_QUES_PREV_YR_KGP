    Behavioral Questions (Situational Judgment)

    Q1. Dealing with Unrealistic Customer Expectations

    Topic: client-management, expectation-setting

    Your team realized that unrealistic customer expectations were being set in the sales process, leading to over-servicing. Over-servicing is affecting your organization's growth due to time spent doing unpaid work. Which of the following would you choose in this situation?

    Options: - A) Roll out an incentive program for the team to stop overservicing. - B) Start charging all existing customers for all the over-service, thereby increasing revenues. - C) Implement an overage policy to set service limits and provide a framework for exception cases. - D) Deny overservicing to any customer going forward with immediate effect.

    Correct Answer: C) Implement an overage policy to set service limits and provide a framework for exception cases. Explanation: Implementing a structured overage policy sets professional boundaries, defines service limits clearly, and provides a systematic way to handle exceptions. This protects the company from unpaid work while maintaining customer trust, rather than abruptly denying service or penalizing the team.

    Q2. Implementing Process Improvements

    Topic: change-management, team-leadership

    You are working as a Quality Control manager overseeing production at a large manufacturing company. Recently, a new initiative was taken up to improve production processes, and an email was sent to all the employees communicating the changes made. Some employees in the production department are still not used to the process improvements implemented. A few of them are rigid and continue to follow the earlier process. What would you do to achieve progress in implementing the improved process?

    Options: - A) Roll out a communication and training plan to reach out to the employees in the production department. - B) Resend emails to all the employees, and set up posters on the production floor to reach out to everyone in the organisation. - C) Seek support from the Human Resources department to lead the change. - D) Talk to each employee one-on-one in the production department to address their challenges in adapting to the change in the process.

    Correct Answer: A) Roll out a communication and training plan to reach out to the employees in the production department. Explanation: A comprehensive communication and structured training plan targets the root cause of the issue (lack of familiarity and skills). It helps the production employees understand the benefits and details of the new process, which is more effective than simply resending emails or posters.

    Q3. Prioritizing Urgent Business Calls

    Topic: prioritization, crisis-management

    You are on your way to your organisation's Strategy meet, which happens once every two years. Just before you are about to board your flight, you get two calls. One is from a long-term customer (whose office is near the airport) who has escalated the matter of your team not meeting the SLA on the latest project, which generally does not happen. The second is from your Finance team, who wants to immediately discuss pending cases of Accounts Receivables. What would your next step be?

    Options: - A) Skip the Strategy meet and head to the customer site immediately, in parallel ask your Finance team to postpone the meeting to the next day so that you can be present. - B) Skip the Strategy meet and head back to the office to discuss the Receivables issue, as cash flow is critical for business; ask your account manager to visit the customer. - C) Skip the Strategy meet and head to the customer site immediately, as you always put customer needs first; ask your team to handle the Receivables discussion. - D) Proceed to the Strategy meet without changing your plans, and ask your team to handle both the issues.

    Correct Answer: D) Proceed to the Strategy meet without changing your plans, and ask your team to handle both the issues. Explanation: As a leader, you must trust and delegate operational tasks to your team, especially when attending a rare and critical event like a biennial Strategy meet. Both issues (an SLA violation and Accounts Receivables) are operational and can be handled by the respective teams in your absence.

    Q4. Product Launch Strategy for Niche Markets

    Topic: business-strategy, marketing

    You work in a team that supports small businesses to build and strengthen their business strategy. A Tea estate owner is your new customer with a business plan to sell Tea online, operating from a store near the estate. The owner wants to build a business to last for a few generations in the family and be a distinct market player rather than making a huge profit. A market research study was held in a nearby city with fifteen flavours of tea from the owner's estate. The study found that six flavours were to the liking of most tasters. Two flavours were unique to this estate, which were hard to produce and available only in specific seasons. Which of the following would you recommend for the online business to run in profit?

    Options: - A) Focus on premium packaging with only the two unique flavours as limited editions. - B) Focus on a limited edition launch for the two unique flavours and six flavours liked by most tasters. - C) Focus on premium packaging and launch with all fifteen flavours. - D) Focus on the six flavours liked by most tasters and market all of them as distinct flavours.

    Correct Answer: B) Focus on a limited edition launch for the two unique flavours and six flavours liked by most tasters. Explanation: This hybrid approach balances volume and exclusivity. The six popular flavours ensure stable sales, while the two unique seasonal flavours build a distinct brand identity and prestige without overstretching production capabilities.

    Q5. Handling Manager's Influence on Surveys

    Topic: ethics, team-management

    Your organization is conducting an employee survey to identify the top three areas that the organization can improve upon. The most requested areas will be taken ahead for implementation. Your manager has shared his list openly and asked everyone in your team to submit the same so that those suggestions come out on top and are prioritised. Which course of action would you choose next?

    Options: - A) Talk to the rest of the team, and make suggestions based on what everyone feels is important. - B) Ignore the survey, as most such employee surveys do not result in anything. - C) Follow your manager's direction and make the same suggestions. - D) Make suggestions based on your own views without consulting anyone.

    Correct Answer: D) Make suggestions based on your own views without consulting anyone. Explanation: An employee survey is designed to capture honest, individual feedback to improve the organization. Copying the manager's list defeats the purpose of the survey and compromises its integrity. Submitting your own views independently is the ethical and correct course of action.

    Q6. Handling Non-Cooperative Business Partners

    Topic: partner-management, conflict-resolution

    Mr Paul is a regional sales partner for your organisation. He has been with your company since your CEO started the business two decades ago and knows the CEO personally. While he was a large partner in the beginning, as your organisation has grown, his contribution has become smaller. Of late, Paul has not been co-operating, which is draining your efforts to build your organization's brand. Multiple directives asking for improvement have yielded no results. You have to now decide whether or not to continue working with Mr Paul, which may impact the reputation of your organization and effort in finding a replacement. Which of the following will you choose as your immediate next step?

    Options: - A) Continue working with Paul as is because he knows the CEO and any action you take may backfire on you. - B) Send a detailed directive to Paul explaining the expected behaviour from him. - C) Call for an in-person meeting with Paul, and ask the CEO to join the meeting to avoid any issues later on. - D) Discontinue working with Paul immediately after providing adequate notice.

    Correct Answer: C) Call for an in-person meeting with Paul, and ask the CEO to join the meeting to avoid any issues later on. Explanation: Since Paul has a long-standing personal relationship with the CEO and his departure could impact the company's reputation, involving the CEO in a direct, face-to-face alignment meeting is the most diplomatic first step. It ensures transparency, respects the relationship, and prepares the ground for a smooth transition if partnership termination becomes necessary.

    Q7. Handling Unmet Milestones in Key Projects

    Topic: project-management, client-relations

    You have been appointed as a project manager for a client facing project. The client is one of the largest customers for your organisation and is also quite profitable. You have put in place a 45-day plan to meet the required timelines without any compromise in quality. However, at the halfway stage, many milestones are unmet due to some external circumstances. Select which action would you choose at this stage of the project.

    Options: - A) Have an update meeting with the customer and let them know the revised timelines, but do not compromise on quality. - B) Continue with the existing plan but push the team to work harder and track progress closely. - C) Add additional resources, which increases your costs but ensures the project is delivered on time with the right quality. - D) Review which tasks can be deprioritised even if quality suffers a little, and remove them from the task list so that you can meet the timelines.

    Correct Answer: A) Have an update meeting with the customer and let them know the revised timelines, but do not compromise on quality. Explanation: Transparency is key in client relationships, especially with key accounts. Informing the client of external delays and presenting realistic, revised timelines without compromising quality protects long-term trust and deliverable integrity.

    Q8. Resolving Interpersonal Conflict with Colleagues

    Topic: interpersonal-skills, conflict-resolution

    One of your colleagues has stopped interacting with you after an argument about project deliverables. You two were quite friendly before that day. You believe that something you said may have hurt the colleague personally, even though you did not intend it so. Which one of the following will you choose to do?

    Options: - A) Ask your manager to intervene to set the relationship right. - B) Apologise to the colleague for any personal remarks and hope the colleague reciprocates. - C) Send a detailed email to the colleague explaining your position on the project deliverables, and hope that logic prevails. - D) Give it a few days for things to cool down, and make a gesture of peace such as inviting the colleague for coffee.

    Correct Answer: D) Give it a few days for things to cool down, and make a gesture of peace such as inviting the colleague for coffee. Explanation: Giving the colleague some space allows emotions to cool down. A friendly, informal gesture like inviting them for coffee creates a low-pressure environment to clear up misunderstandings and rebuild the personal connection.

    Q9. Policy Changes During Active Negotiations

    Topic: integrity, client-relations

    During negotiations for a contract renewal, you commit to a customer for a 15% price reduction in exchange for growth in the business volume. As per the organisation's approval matrix, you are authorised to approve a price reduction of up to 20%. However, before you can get the contract closed, a new organisation policy is announced, which restricts price reduction to 10% for any customer. What would your approach be to the commitment you already made?

    Options: - A) Reach out to the Finance team and ask them to back-date the 15% reduction in exchange for a favour. - B) Go back to the customer and inform them of the policy change, and try to negotiate for a 10% reduction only. - C) Restructure the deal by breaking the contract into two parts and showing them as a new contract which is not bound by the new policy, thereby keeping your commitment. - D) Write to senior management to get approval as an exceptional case, since you already made the commitment.

    Correct Answer: D) Write to senior management to get approval as an exceptional case, since you already made the commitment. Explanation: Seeking approval from senior management as an exceptional case demonstrates both professional integrity (honoring your word to the client) and respect for company policies. Back-dating or breaking contracts are unethical workarounds, and renegotiating immediately damages the company's credibility.

    Q10. Unexpected Resignation during a Specialized Project

    Topic: resource-management, project-continuity

    Your team just started working on a specialised project after receiving the required training. One of the resources, Mr Ram, resigns suddenly with a plan to move to his hometown in 15 days to care for his unwell parents. As the project head, what would you choose to do in this situation?

    Options: - A) Start looking for potential candidates immediately with a focus on readily available candidates, and hire the first decent candidate you find. - B) Distribute Ram's tasks to other team members and ask Ram to train them before leaving. - C) Insist Mr Ram cannot leave before finding a suitable replacement for himself and providing complete training. - D) Negotiate with Mr Ram to work remotely, until the HR team finds a suitable replacement.

    Correct Answer: D) Negotiate with Mr Ram to work remotely, until the HR team finds a suitable replacement. Explanation: Given that the project is specialized and the team just received training, finding and training a replacement in 15 days is highly impractical. Allowing Ram to work remotely to care for his parents supports his personal needs while ensuring project continuity and knowledge preservation.

    Q11. Rebranding vs. Maintaining Brand Values

    Topic: brand-strategy, marketing

    Your organization is the market leader in an industry where the quality of products matters a lot, and you enjoy a strong brand reputation. Your smaller competitors have recently rebranded themselves and have launched an aggressive marketing campaign, in an effort to gain market share. There are growing murmurs within your company that you should rebrand to show to the market that you are also trendy. What would you recommend to your management?

    Options: - A) Go ahead with the rebranding because change is what the market expects. - B) Reach out to all your existing customers and offer discounts. - C) Increase your marketing spends but continue with your brand values as they have worked in the past. - D) Do not make any changes as it may confuse the existing clients.

    Correct Answer: C) Increase your marketing spends but continue with your brand values as they have worked in the past. Explanation: Rebranding a market leader with a strong reputation just because competitors are doing so is risky and unnecessary. Instead, increasing marketing spend to reaffirm and highlight your core brand values (like quality) will counter the competitors' campaigns without diluting your established brand equity.

    Q12. Managing Team Promotion & Resource Allocation

    Topic: team-leadership, resource-management

    Your manager has announced a new project may come to your team as final discussions are underway with the customer. It is a big project with high visibility, and can set you up for a promotion. Your team is already busy working on multiple projects, but you have the required technical capabilities. As a team leader, what would you want to know first when the announcement is made?

    Options: - A) The size of the deal and the revenue the project will bring in for your organisation. - B) Know what support you can expect from the organisation to complete the project. - C) The duration and deliverables of the project, to be sure you can deliver. - D) Know which other teams are competing for the project, so that you can talk to your manager to improve your chances of getting the project.

    Correct Answer: C) The duration and deliverables of the project, to be sure you can deliver. Explanation: As a team leader, your primary responsibility is delivery and quality. Understanding the project duration and deliverables first allows you to assess resource feasibility and plan capacity, ensuring you do not overcommit a busy team.

    Q13. Rewarding the Team & Trip Planning

    Topic: team-engagement, operational-efficiency

    Your 30-member team has exceeded the annual targets, and as a reward, the senior management has approved a foreign trip. A destination has been decided, which is new to all of you. Which one of the following would you choose for everyone to have a great travel experience?

    Options: - A) Hire a professional travel agent to plan the entire trip, even if it costs more. - B) Ask one of the junior team members to take some time out to plan the trip. - C) Call the entire team for a few meetings to jointly discuss and finalise the details. - D) Request a friend, who runs a visa arrangement business, to plan the trip as a personal favour.

    Correct Answer: A) Hire a professional travel agent to plan the entire trip, even if it costs more. Explanation: Planning a foreign trip for a large group of 30 people is a complex task. Hiring a professional travel agent ensures a hassle-free, well-organized experience, allowing the team to fully enjoy their reward without burdening internal team members.

    Q14. Appointing Deputy Project Manager

    Topic: leadership, conflict-avoidance

    You have been recently appointed as the manager of a new project. The general norm in the company is to have a team leader who works as a deputy to the project manager. There are only two worthy candidates who suit the requirement, however, you suspect that appointing one as the team leader will discourage the other and stop them from giving their best. What would you do under these circumstances?

    Options: - A) Decide not to announce a team leader at the start of the project, and keep watching both the candidates' performance. - B) Pick one of the two who you think is a better candidate and make the decision public. - C) Ignore both candidates and pick a third candidate which will avoid the conflict. - D) Tell your preferred candidate your decision privately so as to avoid the other candidate being disappointed.

    Correct Answer: A) Decide not to announce a team leader at the start of the project, and keep watching both the candidates' performance. Explanation: Delaying the appointment allows both candidates to showcase their capabilities on the live project. This keeps them motivated, promotes healthy competition, and gives you more objective performance data to make the final decision.

    Q15. Dealing with an Out-of-Character Outburst

    Topic: empathy, team-management

    One of your colleagues said something rude while addressing the team in a general conversation at work. This colleague was generally known to be a soft spoken person and had not exhibited similar behavior in the past. As a team member, what will you do next?

    Options: - A) Bring it up in the next meeting so that the rest of the team does not commit a similar mistake. - B) Talk to the colleague separately to understand the reason behind the outburst, and offer any help if needed. - C) Probe if the colleague was undergoing any stressful situation which may have led to the incident. - D) Ignore it, as it was a one off and the colleague was probably just blowing off some steam.

    Correct Answer: B) Talk to the colleague separately to understand the reason behind the outburst, and offer any help if needed. Explanation: Since this behavior is out of character, a private, empathetic conversation helps you understand if they are facing personal or professional stress, allowing you to offer support rather than alienating them or publicizing the slip-up.

    Q16. Responding to Senior Management Dissatisfaction

    Topic: communication, responsibility

    You receive an email from your senior management expressing their dissatisfaction with a recent project delivered by your team. Your team manager is on leave for a week. As a senior member of the team, which one of the following would you choose to do?

    Options: - A) Schedule a meeting of the senior management team with the entire project team so that each team member gets an opportunity to present their viewpoint. - B) Respond immediately with an apology email. - C) Discuss with the team internally and respond with a short note highlighting the issues in the project. - D) Wait for the manager to come back from leave and respond, as you responding might make the issue bigger.

    Correct Answer: C) Discuss with the team internally and respond with a short note highlighting the issues in the project. Explanation: As a senior team member, taking ownership by acknowledging the feedback, discussing it internally with the team to identify gaps, and sending a concise response showing action is the most professional path. Waiting a week or sending an empty apology shows lack of proactiveness.

    Q17. Assisting Customers Outside Assigned Role

    Topic: customer-first, proactiveness

    Your company is celebrating "Foundation Day" and has invited a few customers to your office. Some employees have been given the opportunity to take care of customers on that day. You too wanted that opportunity but got missed out. On "Foundation Day", you reach the office early and notice one of the customers (who was also early) was wandering about the lobby area, unsure of what to do. One of your colleagues was supposed to take care of this customer. Which of the following do you choose to do?

    Options: - A) Introduce yourself to the customer and keep them company until your colleague arrives. - B) Proceed as usual to your work, as you are not aware of the special arrangements made for customers. - C) Inform security to escort the customer to a meeting room and ask them to wait until your colleague arrives. - D) Call your colleague to update that their customer had reached, and ask the colleague to reach the office immediately.

    Correct Answer: A) Introduce yourself to the customer and keep them company until your colleague arrives. Explanation: Putting the customer first and ensuring they feel welcomed reflects positively on the organization. Keeping them company is proactive, hospitable, and handles the situation professionally until the designated colleague arrives.

    Q18. Last-Minute Complex Change Requests

    Topic: client-relations, project-integrity

    You work for a technology company which builds websites for customers. On one particular project, you received a last-minute change request from the client just one day before the launch of their website. The client expects to launch the site with the change implemented. The change however is complex and can be released only after intense testing, which is not possible with your current team. Which of the following do you choose to do?

    Options: - A) Deny the client's request, as it is risky to make changes at the last minute without delaying the launch. - B) Implement the change before the launch date without doing the required testing. - C) Initiate the change implementation and let the client know the website launch is delayed. - D) Implement the change by pulling in more manpower to work on the testing, even though it is not approved in your organisation's budget.

    Correct Answer: C) Initiate the change implementation and let the client know the website launch is delayed. Explanation: Implementing untested complex changes on launch day is extremely risky and can lead to site crashes. The best path is to agree to the change but communicate clearly that a delay is required to ensure proper quality control and testing.

    Q19. Challenging a Process Change

    Topic: assertiveness, professional-integrity

    After a senior management discussion with the client, your manager asks you to implement a new step in the process of an ongoing project. You are quite sure that this step would interfere with the quality and timeline of completing the project. Which one of the following would you choose to do?

    Options: - A) Agree to implement the new step after a couple of months, but use that time to gather evidence to prove the new step would impact quality. - B) Request for a discussion to explain the possible problems with the new step before doing any implementation. - C) Refuse to implement the new step as it would cause delays. - D) Implement the new step as it is a direct ask from both the client and senior management.

    Correct Answer: B) Request for a discussion to explain the possible problems with the new step before doing any implementation. Explanation: Discussing your concerns proactively and explaining the potential negative impact on quality and timeline is the most constructive response. It allows you to align with your manager before any damage is done, rather than blindly implementing or flatly refusing.

    Q20. Declining Productivity in a Colleague

    Topic: empathy, teamwork

    You have noticed that the productivity of one of your colleagues has recently been dropping off. You get to know that the colleague is going through a stressful time because of a parent's recent diagnosis of a serious medical problem. What would be your next course of action?

    Options: - A) Probe the colleague regarding the nature of the illness and provide references of some experts you may know. - B) Talk to the colleague and explain that personal matters should be kept separate from professional matters. - C) Request your manager to replace the colleague in question until the personal situation improves. - D) Share an upsetting health situation from your own experience and explain how you effectively overcame it.

    Correct Answer: A) Probe the colleague regarding the nature of the illness and provide references of some experts you may know. Explanation: Demonstrating empathy and offering practical help (like expert medical contacts) shows genuine support for a colleague during a difficult time, fostering team bond and assisting them in resolving their personal stress.

    Q21. Angle of Elevation & Speed Ratio

    Topic: trigonometry, relative-speed

    There are two towers AB and CD (A and C being their tops and B and D being their feet). The angle of elevation of C from B is 30° and the angle of elevation of A from D is 60°. An eagle and a kite start flying at the same moment from the tops of towers AB and CD respectively towards the foot of the other tower along a straight line and at uniform speeds. If they meet at one point along their flight, find the ratio of their speeds.

    Options: - A) \(\sqrt{3} : 1\) - B) \(3\sqrt{3} : 1\) - C) \(3\sqrt{3} : 2\) - D) \(3 : 1\)

    Correct Answer: B) \(3\sqrt{3} : 1\)

    Q56. Shadow & Direction Sense

    Topic: direction-sense

    One morning after sunrise, Mike and John were standing in a lawn with their backs towards each other. Mike's shadow fell exactly towards his right hand side. Which direction was John facing?

    Options: - A) East - B) South - C) West - D) North

    Correct Answer: D) North

    Solution: * In the morning, the sun is in the East, so all shadows fall towards the West. * Mike's shadow fell to his right \(\implies\) Mike's right hand points West. * If a person's right hand points West, they are facing South. * Since Mike and John have their backs to each other, John is facing the opposite direction of South, which is North.

    Q58. Ranking Puzzle: Rank Difference

    Correct Answer: D) 4

    Solution: 1. \(C > A > B\) 2. \(F > D > E\) 3. \(F\) is Rank 1. 4. \(E > A\) 5. \(E > C\) Combining all inequalities: $\(F > D > E > C > A > B\)$ Ranks: F(1), D(2), E(3), C(4), A(5), B(6). Difference between A's rank (5) and F's rank (1) is \(5 - 1 = 4\).

    Q64. Inside-Outside Circle Puzzle

    Topic: number-puzzle

    Refer to the double-circle node puzzle. * Top Node: (outside=5, inside=2) * Right Node: (outside=26, inside=5) * Bottom Node: (outside=50, inside=7) * Left Node: (outside=37, inside=?)

    Options: - A) 7 - B) 4 - C) 8 - D) 6

    Correct Answer: D) 6

    Solution: The relation is: \(\text{Outside} = \text{Inside}^2 + 1\). * Top: \(2^2 + 1 = 5\) * Right: \(5^2 + 1 = 26\) * Bottom: \(7^2 + 1 = 50\) * Left: \(?^2 + 1 = 37 \implies ?^2 = 36 \implies ? = 6\).
